About COVID-19

COVID-19, also known as the coronavirus disease, is an infectious respiratory illness caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It was first identified in Wuhan, China in December 2019 and has since spread globally, causing a pandemic.

The symptoms of COVID-19 include:

  • Fever or chills
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Headache
  • New loss of taste or smell
  • Sore throat
  • Congestion or runny nose
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Diarrhea

To prevent the spread of COVID-19, health organizations recommend practicing good hygiene, wearing masks in public, practicing social distancing, and getting vaccinated when available. COVID-19 has had a significant impact on global health, economies, and daily life.
